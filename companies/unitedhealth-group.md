# UnitedHealth Group (UNH)

**Sub-sector:** Payor-provider / managed care (integrated payor + Optum services)
**One-liner:** The largest US healthcare company - a national health insurer (UnitedHealthcare)
fused with a massive care-delivery, pharmacy, and data services arm (Optum) - now working
through a Medicare-Advantage cost shock and a credibility reset.
**Profile last updated:** 2026-06-14

## Business model / how it makes money
Two halves that increasingly feed each other:
- **UnitedHealthcare (insurance):** premiums from MA, Medicaid, commercial, and individual
  plans. Profit = premium minus medical cost (medical care ratio, MCR) minus admin.
- **Optum (services):** Optum Health (care delivery / risk-bearing clinics), Optum Insight
  (data/analytics/tech), Optum Rx (PBM/pharmacy). Fee- and margin-based, and the growth/margin
  engine that differentiates UNH from pure insurers.
The integrated model lets UNH capture the medical dollar at both the insurance and delivery layers.

## Competitive position
- **Humana** - the MA-pure-play rival (UNH is the MA scale leader).
- **Elevance, CVS/Aetna, Cigna** - integrated payor competitors.
- Optum competes with **Astrana, agilon, Privia** (care delivery) and **standalone PBMs** (Optum Rx).

## Recent deal / news (dated)
- **2025:** MA medical-cost spike drove MCR up ~340 bps and multiple guidance resets; **Stephen
  Hemsley returned as CEO** to stabilize; heightened regulatory/DOJ scrutiny of MA practices.
- **Jan 2026:** FY2025 results (adj. EPS $16.35, net income ~$12.1B, both down) and 2026 outlook
  built on repricing (MCR to 88.8%, EPS >$17.75).

## Why it matters for HC coverage
UNH is the bellwether for the entire payor and VBC complex - its MA cost trend, repricing, and
Optum strategy set the tone for the whole sector. The 2025 reset (cost shock + leadership change
+ DOJ overhang) is the defining managed-care story, and Optum is the strategic acquirer shadowing
every diagnostics and VBC asset in this knowledge base.
