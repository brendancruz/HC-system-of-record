# Astrana Health (ASTH)

**Sub-sector:** VBC / integrated care delivery (risk-bearing, tech-enabled)
**One-liner:** A California-rooted, tech-enabled care-delivery platform (formerly Apollo
Medical) profitably scaling value-based care across delegated risk - the operator's
operator in VBC.
**Profile last updated:** 2026-06-14

## Business model / how it makes money
Three segments wrapped around a population-health platform:
- **Care Partners:** the risk-bearing engine - takes delegated/capitated risk from health
  plans across Medicare Advantage, Medicaid, commercial, and ACO lines.
- **Care Delivery:** owned/affiliated medical groups and clinics.
- **Care Enablement:** the technology/MSO platform that runs population health, claims, and
  utilization for its network and third parties.
Profit comes from managing total cost of care below capitation (care margin) plus platform
fees. Unlike agilon's pure MA full-risk model, Astrana is diversified across payer lines and
owns more of the delivery + tech stack, which has kept it profitable and growing.

## Competitive position
- **Privia Health** - capital-light enablement (Astrana takes more delegated risk + owns delivery).
- **agilon health** - MA full-risk (Astrana more diversified and profitable).
- **UnitedHealth (Optum), Humana (CenterWell)** - the payor-owned care-delivery giants.

## Recent deal / news (dated)
- **2025-07-01:** closed **Prospect Health** acquisition (health plan, medical groups in CA/
  TX/AZ/RI, MSO, RightRx pharmacy, Foothill Regional Medical Center) - the FY2025 growth driver.
- **~March 2026:** record FY2025 revenue ($3.18B, +56%); announced share-repurchase program.

## Why it matters for HC coverage
Astrana is the profitable, acquisitive VBC consolidator - it grows by buying and integrating
risk + delivery assets while staying EBITDA-positive and cash-generative, the opposite of the
agilon/P3 full-risk blowups. The cleanest "VBC roll-up that works" story and an active acquirer
to track for advisory flow.
