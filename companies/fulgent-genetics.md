# Fulgent Genetics (FLGT)

**Sub-sector:** Diagnostics (genetic/molecular testing) + early-stage therapeutics
**One-liner:** A post-COVID story stock - a small, growing genetic-testing core plus a
nascent oncology therapeutics arm, sitting on a cash pile larger than its market cap.
**Profile last updated:** 2026-06-13

## Business model / how it makes money
Two distinct pieces:
- **Diagnostics (core):** broad genetic-testing menu - oncology panels, carrier screening
  (Beacon), reproductive (Picture Genetics), rare-disease exome/genome, plus anatomic/
  molecular pathology. Volume x ASP. This is the revenue base after COVID testing rolled off.
- **Therapeutics (development-stage):** a small-molecule oncology pipeline led by FID-007
  (a nanoencapsulated chemotherapy). Pure R&D spend today - no revenue - and a growing cash
  burn the market is watching warily.
The defining feature is the **balance sheet**: Fulgent banked huge COVID-testing profits
and now holds more cash/investments than its entire equity value.

## Valuation snapshot
- Price **$18.68**, market cap **~$0.53B** (FMP, 2026-06-13).
- **The P/S of ~1.6x is misleading.** With ~$755M cash/investments versus a ~$530M market
  cap, **enterprise value is negative** - the market is effectively assigning zero (or less)
  to the operating business and therapeutics pipeline. The real debate is capital allocation:
  buybacks/M&A vs. funding a speculative drug pipeline.

## Competitive position
- **Labcorp, Quest, GeneDx, Natera** - far larger / faster-growing genetic-testing peers.
- Fulgent competes on breadth and price in testing but lacks the scale and growth of the
  category leaders; the therapeutics arm is unproven and sub-scale vs. dedicated biotech.

## Recent deal / news (dated)
- **2025:** FY2025 revenue $322.7M (+14%); guidance raised multiple times during the year.
- **Q1 2026:** posted a loss, reaffirmed ~$350M FY2026 revenue outlook; therapeutics spend
  set to roughly double.

## Why it matters for HC coverage
Fulgent is the **net-cash / capital-allocation** case in diagnostics - a textbook example of
why screening market cap against EV matters. The investable question is what management does
with a cash hoard that exceeds the equity value, which makes it both a potential acquirer and
an activist/value target. Useful as the "EV vs. P/S" teaching name in any comp discussion.
