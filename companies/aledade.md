# Aledade (Private)

**Sub-sector:** VBC / independent-PCP ACO enablement (capital-light)
**One-liner:** The largest network of independent primary-care practices in value-based care -
the private benchmark for the capital-light, ACO/shared-savings model.
**Profile last updated:** 2026-06-14

## Business model / how it makes money
Aledade partners with independent primary-care practices and organizes them into Accountable
Care Organizations (ACOs), then provides the technology, data, and contracting that let those
practices succeed under value-based contracts (Medicare Shared Savings Program, MA, Medicaid,
commercial). Aledade's revenue is primarily a **share of the shared savings** it generates for
practices (aligned, capital-light upside) plus SaaS/enablement fees. Same philosophical model
as Privia (upside-share, not full insurance risk), but private and PCP-network-centric.

## Valuation snapshot
- **Private; last marked at ~$3.5B (June 2023).** No public multiple. If 2024 revenue is
  ~$750M (est.), that is ~4.7x sales on the 2023 mark - but the model (capital-light, fast-
  growing, profitable shared savings) would screen closer to enablement peers like Privia,
  not full-risk names. Treat the $3.5B as a stale floor reference.

## Competitive position
- **Privia Health** - the closest public comp (capital-light enablement; Aledade is the
  private equivalent, more PCP-network-weighted).
- **agilon, Astrana, P3** - full-risk VBC (different, heavier risk model).
- **UnitedHealth (Optum)** - the strategic gravity well for all independent-physician assets.

## Recent deal / news (dated)
- **June 2023:** $260M Series F at ~$3.5B valuation (Lightspeed-led).
- Continued the largest US independent-PCP ACO network; a frequently cited IPO candidate.

## Why it matters for HC coverage
Aledade is the private flagship of capital-light VBC and a perennial IPO/strategic-interest
name. It is the reference point for valuing private physician-enablement assets and the most
likely large VBC IPO to watch - directly comparable to Privia for framing the public-vs-private
enablement spread.
