# Guardant Health (GH)

**Sub-sector:** Diagnostics (liquid biopsy / precision oncology + screening)
**One-liner:** Blood-based precision oncology leader (Guardant360) now scaling a
colorectal-cancer screening test (Shield) into a second, larger growth engine.
**Profile last updated:** 2026-06-13

## Business model / how it makes money
Volume x ASP across three lines:
- **Oncology** (core, ~70% of revenue): Guardant360 liquid-biopsy tests for advanced
  cancer (therapy selection) and Reveal (MRD / recurrence monitoring). Reimbursed by
  Medicare and commercial payors.
- **Biopharma & Data:** testing services and data/analytics sold to pharma for trials
  and companion-diagnostic development. Higher-margin, lumpier.
- **Screening:** Shield, a blood-based CRC screening test - the high-optionality bet
  on a huge asymptomatic-screening TAM. ~87k Shield tests in FY2025.

- Price/Sales: ~$17.45B / ~$973M FY2025 = **~18x** trailing; ~$17.45B / ~$1.31B 2026E

  screening optionality on top of a 25%+ growing oncology base.

## Competitive position
- **Exact Sciences (now Abbott) / Shield vs. Cologuard** - the headline blood-vs-stool
  CRC screening battle.
- **Natera, Tempus** - overlapping in liquid biopsy / MRD (Reveal vs. Signatera).
- **Foundation Medicine (Roche)** - tissue + liquid comprehensive genomic profiling.
- **Grail** - adjacent in multi-cancer early detection.

## Recent deal / news (dated)
- **Q1 2026 (reported ~May 2026):** raised full-year 2026 revenue guidance; Shield
  scaling as the screening story.
- Shield blood-based CRC screening continues to build payor coverage and guideline
  traction - the key catalyst to watch.

## Why it matters for HC coverage
GH is the cleanest public read on "blood-based screening optionality" - it trades at a
double-digit sales multiple largely on Shield's TAM, not current cash flow. Useful as
the high-multiple anchor in any diagnostics comp conversation and the direct competitive
counter-narrative to the Exact Sciences/Abbott franchise.
