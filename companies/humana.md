# Humana (HUM)

**Sub-sector:** Payor-provider / managed care (Medicare Advantage pure-play + CenterWell)
**One-liner:** The Medicare Advantage pure-play - the most MA-levered large payor, working
through membership declines and a star-ratings dispute that directly hits 2026 economics.
**Profile last updated:** 2026-06-14

## Business model / how it makes money
Predominantly **Medicare Advantage** premiums (the most MA-concentrated of the big payors),
plus a fast-growing healthcare-services arm (**CenterWell** - primary care, pharmacy, home
health). Insurance profit = premium minus medical cost (benefit ratio) minus admin; MA economics
hinge heavily on **CMS Star Ratings**, which drive quality-bonus payments. CenterWell adds
owned care delivery (the Optum-style vertical integration play).

## Valuation snapshot
- Price **$379.22**, market cap **~$45.5B** (FMP, 2026-06-14). 52-week range $163.11 - $379.50 -
  a large recovery off the 2025 lows.
- P/E ~22x FY2025 adj. EPS (~$17.00). Mid-pack payor multiple - depressed earnings base (MA
  margin pressure + star-ratings overhang) keeps the P/E optically high on trough earnings.

## Competitive position
- **UnitedHealth (UnitedHealthcare)** - the MA scale leader and primary rival.
- **CVS/Aetna, Elevance, Centene** - MA / government-program competitors.
- CenterWell competes with **Optum Health, Astrana, agilon** in senior-focused care delivery.

## Recent deal / news (dated)
- **2025:** Humana **sued CMS to vacate its 2025 Star Ratings** - if unsuccessful, lower stars
  cut 2026 quality-bonus revenue, a material overhang on 2026 earnings.
- **2025:** individual MA membership declined ~425k as Humana exited unprofitable plans/markets
  to protect margin; adj. EPS guidance raised to ~$17.00.

## Why it matters for HC coverage
Humana is the cleanest read on **Medicare Advantage** specifically - no commercial cushion, so
its benefit ratio, membership, and star-ratings outcome are the purest signal on MA profitability.
The star-ratings litigation is a sector-defining regulatory story, and Humana is a perennial
strategic/consolidation talking point (recall the prior Cigna-Humana speculation).
