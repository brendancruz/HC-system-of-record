# Tempus AI (TEM)

**Sub-sector:** Diagnostics + healthcare data/AI
**One-liner:** An AI-driven precision-medicine platform pairing high-growth genomic
diagnostics with a differentiated de-identified clinical-genomic data business sold to
pharma.
**Profile last updated:** 2026-06-13

## Business model / how it makes money
Two engines:
- **Genomics / Diagnostics** (~75% of revenue): oncology sequencing, hereditary testing
  (boosted by the Ambry Genetics acquisition), molecular/anatomic pathology. Volume x ASP.
- **Data & Applications:** "Insights" - licensing a large library of de-identified,
  multimodal (clinical + molecular + imaging) data to pharma/biotech, plus Trials
  (clinical-trial matching) and algorithmic tests. Higher-margin, more durable, and the
  strategic differentiator vs. pure testing labs.

## Valuation snapshot
- Price **$47.82**, market cap **~$8.35B** (FMP, 2026-06-13). High beta (~3.6).
- Price/Sales: ~$8.35B / ~$1.27B FY2025 = **~6.6x** trailing; ~$8.35B / ~$1.59B 2026E
  = **~5.3x** forward. (Carries debt from acquisitions; true EV/Sales a touch higher.)
- Mid-pack on sales multiple despite the fastest growth in the set - the data business
  is the part that could re-rate it if Insights keeps compounding at a software-like margin.

## Competitive position
- **Guardant Health, Natera, Foundation Medicine (Roche)** - genomic profiling / liquid biopsy.
- **Caris Life Sciences (private/recently public)** - the closest "molecular profiling +
  data" comp.
- **Flatiron Health (Roche)** - the incumbent in oncology real-world data, the closest
  comp for the Insights data business.

## Why it matters for HC coverage
Tempus is the marquee "AI + healthcare data" diagnostics name and an active acquirer -
three deals in 2025 alone. The Insights data business is the strategic wildcard: if it's
valued like software rather than a lab, the multiple story changes entirely. A natural
centerpiece for any precision-medicine or health-data coverage pitch.
