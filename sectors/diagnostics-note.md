## Executive summary

Diagnostics is the HC sub-sector where the **valuation question is sharpest**: the same kind
of income statement (high-growth, often pre-profit, reimbursement-dependent) earns anywhere
from **1.6x to ~18x sales** depending entirely on which TAM story the market believes. Two
2026 anchors now frame the whole field:

- **Take-out anchor (~6-7x sales):** Abbott bought Exact Sciences for ~$21B / $105/sh (closed
  2026-03-23) at ~6.5x revenue / ~52x adj. EBITDA - the price a strategic pays for **profitable,
  reimbursed, recurring** screening scale.
- **Optionality premium (double-digit sales):** Guardant (~18x) and Natera (~13x) trade on
  **screening and MRD TAM**, not current cash flow.

Place any dx name on the spectrum between those two poles and you have the fluent read. The
recurring tell to watch across all of them is **reimbursement + guideline news, not assay novelty**.

**Profiled universe (7 names):** ~$9.3B FY2025 revenue, ~$79.9B market cap, ~+30% revenue-weighted
growth, ~8.6x aggregate P/S (as of 2026-06-13; EXAS pinned at deal price).

**The four ideas we'd lead with:** Natera (MRD compounder), Guardant (screening-optionality
anchor), Tempus (data re-rate wildcard), GeneDx (convergence/turnaround) - with NeoGenomics /
Fulgent as a consolidation watch.

## 1. Industry overview

Money is made on **volume x ASP**: a test is ordered, run, reported, and reimbursed (Medicare +
commercial). Defensible economics come from **reimbursement coverage** (a covered rate is the
moat), **guideline inclusion** (drives ordering volume), and **menu/scale** (fixed-cost lab
leverage). Gross margins in the universe run ~38% to ~74%, widening with molecular mix and scale.

**Growth is barbelled.** A fast cohort (TEM +83% Ambry-inflated/~30% organic, WGS ~+40%,
NTRA +36%, GH +31%) compounds on screening/MRD/exome demand; a slow cohort (EXAS +18%
scaled-and-profitable, FLGT +14%, NEO +10%) is mature lab volume.

**Four functional layers:** (1) screening / asymptomatic population - largest TAM, richest
multiples; (2) precision oncology / liquid biopsy - the commercial core; (3) MRD / recurrence
monitoring - the highest-conviction growth vector after screening; (4) genomic & legacy lab -
turnaround re-rate (WGS) to show-me value (NEO, FLGT). Full detail: [overview](./diagnostics.md).

## 2. Competitive landscape

Competition is fought **per battleground** (clinical use case + payor), not company-vs-company
across the board:

| Battleground | Leader(s) | Challenger(s) |
|---|---|---|
| CRC screening - stool | Exact / Cologuard (Abbott) | - |
| CRC screening - blood | Guardant / Shield | Exact (Cancerguard), Grail (MCED adj.) |
| Liquid biopsy - therapy selection | Guardant360, Foundation (Roche) | Tempus, Natera |
| MRD / recurrence | Natera / Signatera | Guardant Reveal, Tempus |
| Hereditary / exome-genome | GeneDx | Natera, labs |
| Broad / legacy molecular | NeoGenomics, Fulgent | Quest / Labcorp |

**The headline fight is blood-vs-stool CRC screening** - Shield (GH) vs. Cologuard (now Abbott),
convenience vs. sensitivity, now with a startup-vs-large-cap distribution mismatch. **MRD is the
cleanest secular winner** after screening; **the legacy tier is commoditizing** toward
reference-lab economics and is more likely consolidated than re-rated. Adjacent players that
bound the field (not separately profiled): Roche/Foundation, Grail, Quest/Labcorp, Abbott. Full
detail: [landscape](./diagnostics-landscape.md).

## 4. Ideas shortlist

Coverage-prep thesis hooks (not investment recommendations). Full detail + "what to watch":
[ideas](./diagnostics-ideas.md).

1. **Natera (NTRA) - MRD compounder.** Cleanest pure-play on MRD; Signatera driving +36% at
   $2.3B; 13.2x premium is growth-justified. *Watch:* MRD reimbursement breadth.
2. **Guardant (GH) - screening-optionality anchor.** ~18x for Shield blood-based CRC optionality
   on a 25%+ oncology base. *Watch:* Shield payor coverage + guidelines; EBITDA still negative.
3. **Tempus (TEM) - data re-rate wildcard.** +83% (~30% organic) yet only ~6.6x; data/Insights
   is the catalyst. *Watch:* organic growth + data monetization.
4. **GeneDx (WGS) - convergence/turnaround.** Exome/genome +53-55% at ~74% adj. GM, only 4.2x;
   room to converge. *Watch:* sustained growth + reported FY2025 actuals.

**Watch (not a hook):** NeoGenomics / Fulgent - show-me / legacy tier; more likely consolidation
targets than standalone re-rates.

## How to deploy in a coverage conversation

- Open with the **two-anchor frame** (take-out ~6-7x via Abbott/EXAS vs. screening/MRD premium
  via GH/NTRA), then place each name on it in one sentence.
- The differentiated takes are **TEM** (cheap-vs-growth gap, data optionality) and **WGS**
  (convergence candidate) - where you sound like you've done the work.
- The recurring tell is **reimbursement + guidelines**, not assay novelty.
