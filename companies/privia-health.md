# Privia Health (PRVA)

**Sub-sector:** VBC / physician enablement (capital-light)
**One-liner:** A physician-enablement platform that helps independent practices move
into value-based care - asset-light, profitable, and growing, the opposite of the
full-risk MA model.
**Profile last updated:** 2026-06-13

## Business model / how it makes money
Privia partners with independent providers and aggregates them into a single-TIN
medical group + Management Services Organization (MSO) + Accountable Care Organization
(ACO). It makes money from:
- A share of fee-for-service collections it administers for affiliated practices.
- **Value-based care economics:** shared savings and care-management fees from ACO /
  payor contracts (capital-light - Privia mostly takes *upside* shared savings rather
  than full downside insurance risk, which is what keeps margins positive and stable
  vs. full-risk peers like agilon).
- Platform / technology and admin services fees.
Key operating metrics: implemented providers, practice collections, Care Margin,
Platform Contribution.

- Net cash ~$479.7M -> EV ~$2.47B. EV/EBITDA = ~$2.47B / $125.5M FY2025 = **~19.7x**.
- A profitable-VBC multiple: lower than high-growth dx, but the GAAP profitability,
  net-cash balance sheet, and ~39% EBITDA growth justify a premium to loss-making
  full-risk VBC peers.

## Competitive position
Capital-light enablement, distinct from full-risk MA models:
- **agilon health** - full-risk Medicare Advantage (very different risk profile).
- **Aledade (private)** - the closest direct comp in independent-PCP / ACO enablement.
- **Astrana Health, P3 Health Partners** - VBC delivery / risk-bearing.
- **UnitedHealth / Optum** - the strategic acquirer-shaped shadow over all of VBC.

## Why it matters for HC coverage
Privia is the "good VBC" counter-example: capital-light, GAAP-profitable, net cash, and
compounding - it sidesteps the medical-loss-ratio blowups that have hammered full-risk
names. The cleanest case study for why *risk structure* (upside-share vs. full
capitation) drives VBC valuations, and a logical consolidation participant.
