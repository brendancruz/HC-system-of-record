# agilon health (AGL)

**Sub-sector:** VBC / Medicare Advantage (full-risk, capitated)
**One-liner:** A full-risk Medicare Advantage enabler for primary-care physicians -
high-beta turnaround story after medical-cost pressure pushed it to EBITDA losses.
**Profile last updated:** 2026-06-13

## Business model / how it makes money
agilon partners with community primary-care groups and takes them into **global /
full-risk capitation** for Medicare Advantage seniors. It receives the MA premium (PMPM)
from health plans and is responsible for the members' total cost of care. Revenue =
premium dollars; profit = **medical margin** (premium minus medical cost) less
operating cost. Because revenue is mostly pass-through premium, EV/Sales is meaningless
here - the model lives or dies on the **medical loss ratio (MLR)** and medical margin.

The revenue dip and negative medical margin reflect a deliberate retrenchment - exiting
unprofitable markets/partners and re-pricing - amid industry-wide MA cost trend
pressure. Source: company FY2025 release.

## Competitive position
- **Privia Health** - VBC, but capital-light / upside-share (lower risk; contrast).
- **P3 Health Partners, Astrana Health, Clover Health** - risk-bearing VBC / MA peers.
- **Humana, UnitedHealth (Optum)** - the large payors that both *pay* agilon and
  increasingly own competing physician/VBC assets.

## Why it matters for HC coverage
agilon is the cautionary tale of full-risk VBC: when MA cost trend runs hot, full
capitation turns medical margin negative fast. It is the perfect foil to Privia in any
"VBC risk structure" discussion, and a live restructuring/turnaround name - the kind of
situation that generates advisory and financing conversations.
