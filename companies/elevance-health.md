# Elevance Health (ELV)

**Sub-sector:** Payor-provider / managed care (diversified payor + Carelon services)
**One-liner:** The former Anthem - a Blue Cross Blue Shield-anchored diversified payor building
its own Optum-style services arm (Carelon), trading at the cheapest multiple of the big payors.
**Profile last updated:** 2026-06-14

## Business model / how it makes money
- **Health Benefits:** premiums across commercial (large BCBS franchise), Medicaid, Medicare,
  and individual. Profit = premium minus medical cost (benefit expense ratio) minus admin.
- **Carelon:** the services growth engine - Carelon Rx (pharmacy/PBM) and CarelonRx/Carelon
  Health (care delivery, behavioral, specialty) - Elevance's answer to Optum/CenterWell.
Diversification across commercial + government lines gives it more cushion than MA-pure Humana,
but Medicaid (redeterminations) and medical-cost trend have pressured 2025 margins.

## Competitive position
- **UnitedHealth, Humana, CVS/Aetna, Cigna, Centene** - the large-payor peer set.
- Carelon competes with **Optum** and standalone PBMs - the build-out is the key margin story.

## Why it matters for HC coverage
Elevance is the **value / multiple-discount** name among big payors and the second-biggest
"build-your-own-Optum" story (Carelon). Useful as the cheap-payor anchor in any managed-care
comp and the read on commercial + Medicaid trend (versus Humana's pure-MA signal).
