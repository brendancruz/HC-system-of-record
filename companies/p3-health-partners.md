# P3 Health Partners (PIII)

**Sub-sector:** VBC / Medicare Advantage (full-risk) - distressed / turnaround
**One-liner:** A full-risk MA population-health manager in financial distress - going-concern
flagged, post reverse-split, shrinking membership, the cautionary extreme of the VBC cohort.
**Profile last updated:** 2026-06-14

## Business model / how it makes money
Physician-led, full-risk capitation for Medicare Advantage seniors (similar model to agilon):
takes the MA premium and owns total-cost-of-care risk through a network of clinics and
affiliated providers. Profit depends entirely on keeping medical cost below premium (medical
margin). Like agilon, revenue is mostly pass-through premium, so EV/Sales is meaningless - the
story is solvency and whether medical margin can turn positive before the balance sheet gives out.

## Valuation snapshot
- Price **$12.23**, market cap **~$40M** (FMP, 2026-06-14) - post a **1-for-50 reverse split
  (2025-04-11)** done to stay above Nasdaq's $1.00 minimum bid; still flagged delisting risk.
- No meaningful multiple - negative EBITDA, distressed equity. This trades as a deep-distress
  option on a 2026 turnaround to roughly breakeven EBITDA, not on fundamentals.

## Competitive position
- **agilon health** - the larger, better-capitalized full-risk MA peer (also struggled, but solvent).
- **Astrana, Privia** - profitable VBC operators that illustrate what P3 is not.
- Payors (Humana, UNH) - both counterparties and the deep-pocketed alternative.

## Why it matters for HC coverage
P3 is the distressed bookend of VBC - the live case study in how full-risk MA plus leverage
plus star-ratings/cost pressure can push a public company to the brink. Relevant for
restructuring/special-situations conversations and as the cautionary contrast to Astrana's
profitable model in any VBC pitch.
