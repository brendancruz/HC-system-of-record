# Diagnostics - Ideas Shortlist

**Sub-sector:** Diagnostics
**Theme / angle:** Reimbursed, recurring molecular testing re-rating around two TAMs -
asymptomatic **screening** and **MRD** - while large-cap medtech sets the take-out floor (~6-7x sales).
**Companion to:** [overview](./diagnostics.md) · [landscape](./diagnostics-landscape.md) · [comps](../comps/diagnostics.md)
**Shortlist last updated:** 2026-06-14

These are **coverage-prep thesis hooks** (the names that best express the theme and how to
frame them), not investment recommendations. Every hook is grounded in the profiled data;
each "what to watch" is the catalyst that would confirm or break it.

---

## The shortlist (4 names + 1 watch)

### 1. Natera (NTRA) - the MRD compounder
**Hook:** The cleanest pure-play on the MRD TAM - Signatera is driving +36% growth at $2.3B
revenue, the largest market cap in the set, and the premium (13.2x P/S) is growth-justified
rather than pure optionality.
**Why it expresses the theme:** recurring, per-patient, evidence-gated molecular testing - the
secular winner after screening.
**What to watch:** MRD reimbursement breadth and competitive evidence (Reveal, Tempus); any
deceleration de-rates a 13x name fast.

### 2. Guardant Health (GH) - the screening-optionality anchor
**Hook:** The highest-multiple name (~18x trailing / ~13x fwd) because the market is paying
for Shield blood-based CRC screening optionality *on top of* a 25%+ growing oncology base.
**Why it expresses the theme:** the purest read on "blood-based screening TAM premium" and the
direct competitive counter to the Abbott/Cologuard franchise.
**What to watch:** Shield payor coverage + guideline traction - the entire optionality leg
rests on it. EBITDA still negative.

### 3. Tempus AI (TEM) - the data re-rate wildcard
**Hook:** Fastest grower (+83%, ~30% organic) yet only ~6.6x sales - the gap is the question.
The data/Insights business is the potential re-rate catalyst that would separate it from a
pure testing multiple.
**Why it expresses the theme:** tests the "data layer on top of reimbursed testing" extension
of the thesis.
**What to watch:** organic (ex-Ambry) growth and data-business monetization; margin is blended/est.

### 4. GeneDx (WGS) - the convergence/turnaround play
**Hook:** Exome/genome revenue +53-55% at ~74% adj. GM, but trades at just 4.2x - cheapest of
the growth cohort, with room to converge toward the leaders if growth holds.
**Why it expresses the theme:** high-growth reimbursed molecular testing not yet awarded a
growth multiple.
**What to watch:** sustained exome/genome growth and reported (vs. guided) FY2025 revenue;
re-rate is the thesis, so any growth wobble is the risk.

### Watch (not a hook yet): NeoGenomics (NEO) / Fulgent (FLGT)
The show-me / legacy tier (~2x and ~1.6x). FLGT's P/S is **misleading** - net cash exceeds
market cap, so true EV/Sales is ~0; the real question is capital deployment, not the testing
multiple. More likely **consolidation targets** than standalone re-rates.

## How to deploy the shortlist in a coverage conversation

- Lead with the **two-anchor frame** (take-out ~6-7x via Abbott/EXAS vs. screening/MRD premium
  via GH/NTRA), then place each name on it in one sentence.
- The differentiated takes are **TEM** (cheap-vs-growth gap, data optionality) and **WGS**
  (convergence candidate) - those are where you sound like you've done the work.
- The recurring tell across all of them is **reimbursement + guidelines**, not assay novelty.
