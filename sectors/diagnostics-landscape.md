# Diagnostics - Competitive Landscape

**Sub-sector:** Diagnostics (cancer screening, liquid biopsy, MRD, genomic/molecular testing)
**Companion to:** [sector overview](./diagnostics.md) and [comps](../comps/diagnostics.md)
**Landscape last updated:** 2026-06-14
**Universe:** the 7 profiled names + the adjacent players that bound the field.

Competition in dx is fought per **battleground** (a clinical use case + payor), not company
vs. company across the board. The same name can lead one battleground and be a follower in
another. Below: the battlegrounds, who holds each, and the basis of competition.

---

## The battlegrounds (who holds what)

| Battleground | Leader(s) | Challenger(s) | Basis of competition |
|---|---|---|---|
| **CRC screening - stool** | Exact Sciences / Cologuard (Abbott) | - | Installed base, guideline inclusion, profitability |
| **CRC screening - blood** | Guardant / Shield | Exact (Cancerguard pipeline), Grail (MCED adjacent) | Payor coverage + guideline traction; convenience vs. sensitivity |
| **Liquid biopsy - therapy selection** | Guardant360, Foundation (Roche) | Tempus, Natera | Menu breadth, turnaround time, biopharma relationships |
| **MRD / recurrence monitoring** | Natera / Signatera | Guardant Reveal, Tempus | Clinical evidence, tumor-informed vs. -naive, reimbursement breadth |
| **Hereditary / exome-genome** | GeneDx | Natera (women's health genetics), labs | Test menu + interpretation depth, ASP, turnaround |
| **Broad / legacy molecular lab** | NeoGenomics, Fulgent | reference labs (Quest/Labcorp) | Scale, cost per test, menu; commoditizing |

## Positioning map (two axes that matter)

The dx debate collapses onto two axes: **growth/optionality** (is there a screening or MRD
TAM story) vs. **scale/profitability** (is the franchise cash-generative and reimbursed).

- **High optionality, pre-profit:** Guardant (Shield), Natera (Signatera). Richest multiples
  (GH ~18x, NTRA ~13x P/S). The market is paying for TAM, not cash flow.
- **Scaled & profitable:** Exact Sciences (Cologuard, $400M adj. EBITDA) - the take-out
  benchmark, now inside Abbott. The reference point for "what reimbursed scale is worth."
- **High growth, re-rate candidates:** Tempus (data/Insights optionality on +83% Ambry-inflated
  revenue), GeneDx (exome/genome +53-55% turnaround).
- **Show-me value / legacy:** NeoGenomics (~2x), Fulgent (~1.6x, net-cash-distorted) - slower,
  lower-margin, commoditizing menu.

## Adjacent players that bound the field (not profiled in /companies/)

- **Roche / Foundation Medicine** - tissue + liquid comprehensive genomic profiling; the
  large-cap counterweight in therapy-selection. Strategic acquirer template.
- **Grail** - multi-cancer early detection (MCED); adjacent screening TAM, separate clinical
  bet, watched as the long-dated optionality story.
- **Quest Diagnostics / Labcorp** - reference-lab scale; the commoditization floor for legacy
  molecular and the consolidator/distribution partner that can pick up menu.
- **Abbott** - now the scaled-screening owner via Exact Sciences; sets the M&A floor (~6-7x sales).

These are flagged in `_index.md` "Next up" as expansion candidates.

## Recent competitive moves (dated)

- **Abbott / Exact Sciences** (~$21B, closed 2026-03-23) - removes the profitable screening
  incumbent from the public set and gives it large-cap medtech distribution. Raises the bar
  for blood-based challengers (Shield) that must now out-execute an Abbott-backed Cologuard.
  See [deal](../deals/2026-03-abbott-exact-sciences.md).
- **Tempus / Ambry Genetics (+ Deep 6 AI, Paige)** (closed 2025-02-03) - menu + data/AI
  consolidation; inflates Tempus growth optically (~30% organic) while building the data thesis.
  See [deal](../deals/2025-02-tempus-ambry-genetics.md).
- **Guardant Shield** - continuing payor-coverage and guideline build through 2026; the live
  blood-vs-stool CRC screening contest and GH's key catalyst.

## Reads (the banker angle)

- **Reimbursement + guidelines are the real moat**, not assay novelty. The defensible names
  own a covered rate and guideline inclusion (Cologuard, Signatera); challengers live or die
  on coverage build (Shield).
- **The blood-vs-stool CRC screening battle is the headline fight** - Shield (GH) vs. Cologuard
  (now Abbott). Convenience vs. sensitivity, and now a startup-vs-large-cap distribution mismatch.
- **MRD is the cleanest secular winner** after screening - recurring, per-patient, evidence-gated;
  Signatera is the franchise to beat.
- **The legacy lab tier is commoditizing** toward reference-lab economics (NEO, FLGT) - watch
  for them to be consolidated rather than to re-rate on their own.
