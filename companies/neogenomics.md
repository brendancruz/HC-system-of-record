# NeoGenomics (NEO)

**Sub-sector:** Diagnostics (oncology reference lab - clinical + pharma services)
**One-liner:** A cancer-focused reference lab in mid-transition from legacy testing to
NGS - steady low-double-digit growth and modest EBITDA, the "value/show-me" name in the set.
**Profile last updated:** 2026-06-13

## Business model / how it makes money
Volume x ASP across two divisions:
- **Clinical Services** (the bulk): oncology testing for hospitals, pathologists, and
  oncologists - cytogenetics, FISH, flow cytometry, IHC, and a growing **next-generation
  sequencing (NGS)** menu. NGS is the mix-shift story (now ~one-third of clinical revenue,
  growing ~24% while legacy modalities are flat/declining).
- **Pharma Services:** lab services supporting biopharma oncology programs (discovery
  through commercialization) - lumpier, relationship-driven.
Revenue scales with test volume, ASP, and NGS mix; margin expansion depends on moving up
the NGS curve.

## Competitive position
- **Labcorp, Quest Diagnostics** - large reference labs competing in oncology.
- **Tempus, Caris Life Sciences** - NGS-native / data-rich molecular profiling, the
  competitive pressure pushing NEO's mix shift.
- **Guardant, Natera** - liquid-biopsy alternatives to some tissue-based testing.

## Why it matters for HC coverage
NeoGenomics is the **legacy-lab-in-transition** comp - the cheap, slower-growth contrast to
the NGS/MRD high-flyers. It frames the central diagnostics debate: how fast can a traditional
reference lab pivot to NGS before molecular-native players (Tempus, Caris) take the share? A
plausible consolidation candidate given its scale and depressed multiple.
